Synopsis

After reuniting with Gon and his friends, Kurapika explained to them the risks he bears because of his abilities. Believing that his target of revenge is no longer alive and the search for his fallen comrade's eyes could truly begin, Kurapika soon after receives a message informing him that all the Spiders still lived. After much discussion between his friends, Gon, Kurapika and company decided to hunt after the one Spider member who's ability could ultimately result in Kurapika's defeat and death. Based on the manga by Togashi Yoshihiro.

The first OVA continues one of the unresolved plotlines from the 62 episode Hunter x Hunter anime. Kurapica finally reveals to Gon, Killua and Leorio the reason why his nen abilities are so high along with the restrictions on it. He thinks his reason for revenge is done, but it is not - the Spiders are still alive! This OVA focuses on Kurapica's unending quest to avenge his "dead" tribe.

Similar to Hunter x Hunter, the art is good but nothing special although it does continue in the same vein as the original anime so viewers will be used to it. The soundtrack reuses a lot of themes that we're familiar with from the anime also.

It's hard to review this OVA without going into spoilers, but here goes!

The story is wonderful! In my opinion this is the best story among the 3 OVAs. There are twists and turns galore. Added to which the cast of the spiders is amazing - here are bad guys you really want to find out more about. The ending was also ... a surprise! It was a fitting resolution but it leaves the possibility for further plot development open.

Every other episode I find myself thinking "Wow, now THAT is a gifted writer" (who wrote the manga) as he managed to pull together everything perfectly - the pacing, the character development and plot twists!

If you enjoyed Hunter x Hunter you won't want to miss this.

As a continuation of the first series, you can expect the OVA to fill in much of those undesired loose ends and plot holes that cropped up throughout the series. These episodes focus on Kurapica's quest for revenge and his eternal hunt for his clan member's eyes.

Fans of the original series will note that Hunter x Hunter is not a terribly fast-paced anime, and tends to build the story around the characters and their interactions with one another, rather than with action. It lures the audience in by showing them all the interesting depths of the characters -their goals, their dreams, their struggles- and makes us root for them when they hit dirt. This OVA does so much more than that. Following up on the hints we were given with the original series, the OVA gives its audience a much deeper look into Kurapica's emotions and hardships, while also managing to show the heartfelt friendship between him, Leorio, Gon, and Killua.

Kurapica's character, I feel, was for the most part just a supporting character originally and wasn't given too many layers that really fleshed him out and gave the audience much insight into his emotions. Suddenly this problem is completely rectified: he becomes so much more of a main character than I thought he could be, he shows new and interesting sides of himself that I didn't know existed, and he draws you into loving his character so much more so than before. Furthermore, the OVA builds on his friendships and shows the trust, loyalty and support between them.

The Spiders are given stronger personalities and I feel they are much rounder characters than they were before. Each one has a unique relationship with each other and with Kurapica, and you really start to play favourites as he lays out his plan to dissolve the organization.

All in all, for me character was a 10.

The story is these episodes is a bit more fast-paced than what fans are used to (which is a given considering the number of episodes). However, this is what I found so exciting in this OVA. There's a lot of mystery in this plot, and Kurapica's planning skills with his limited number of resources is superb. Just when things start to fall apart, he critically analyzes the situation and pulls everyone through the trouble. I love all the problems Gon and Killua run into, and those two pulled a few stunts to get through them, even surprising many of the Spider members. I must say without a doubt the story was a 10.

As far as art goes, I didn't notice a whole lot of difference between the OVA and the original series. The lines could be cleaner, but that's probably just my preferance rather than the quality of the art. To me it wasn't bad: 8. For sound, in terms of music: it wasn't spectacular but it wasn't terrible either. The opening and ending themes definitely aren't the best, I would say they were average. Background music and sounds were acceptable (but it isn't shinobi battle music, if that's what you're expecting). 7/10 for sound.

To summarize in one short sentence...

This OVA is everything you were expecting and more.

Enjoyment 10
As I stated before, the original Hunter X Hunter Anime ended towards the end of the Yorkshin Arc, cutting off right after the gang finally gets back together. However that wouldn’t be the end of this Version of Hunter X Hunter. Oh no, for as you see, Nippon Animation was far from done with this series. What they decided to do once the televised run was over was to simply switch format, continuing the show in an OVA format instead. Hell, the Episode count doesn’t even reset with these OVAs, so you could just consider them Episodes 61-92 of the TV Series, which is how I’m gonna handle things. So my friends, welcome, to the finale of Yorkshin City.

5th Arc: Yorkshin City: The Final Chapter (Episodes 63-70):

The end of Yorkshin is a bit different than the rest of the Arc. For the most part, the Arc was about the cast trying to find out information about the Gen’Ei Ryodan all the while having to deal with a bunch of other issues. Here, however, while most of those issues have been cleaned up, the cast is under a time limit, so they’re basically in a race against time to see how many of the spider’s legs they can cut while they still have a chance, as its clear it will be a while before they’re able to deal significant damage to the group ever again.

The way it’s done so is also great. Once our heroes’ Plan A fails, they basically have to force the Spiders into a situation in which they’re forced to agree to their demands. In turn, for once, our heroes become the boogeymen, with our villains being the ones to be driven into a corner. It’s especially ironic as even though, as Chrollo himself points out, this should be an epic fail on our heroes side of things, yet it ends up devolving into a mini-internal conflict on the Ryodan’s side of things.

But of course, the heart and soul of the final part of Yorkshin are Pakunoda and Kurapika. Both, ironically, have the same conflict going on. On Pakunoda’s side of things, she needs to decide whether she wants to be loyal to the Spiders or to save her boss. Even though Chrollo himself tells her to do the latter, she decides not to. Who cares if she’s gonna betray the Ryodan by doing this? She’s gonna save Chrollo no matter what. By the end, you can’t help but feel sad about her, which is ironic given that she’s a cold-blooded killer.

Similarly, Kurapika ain’t doing much better. He’s literally at the point that he needs to be in order to accomplish his revenge. The event he’s spent his whole life waiting for is here, the one he wants to kill above all else is finally at her grasps, yet he’s forced into an ultimatum: If he goes through with it, his friends will die. He just keeps looking for ways to both kill Chrollo and save Killua and Gon, yet by the end, he can’t find it. So in the end, he comes to his ultimate answer: Screw it. Screw Chrollo, screw getting revenge, screw everything! He’s lost all of his friends once already, he’s not gonna lose them again.

My only slight complaint with these OVA is a really minor one: There’s a scene towards the end of the Yorkshin Arc in all versions in which Hisoka tries to challenge Chrollo into a fight. Now in both the Manga and 2011 Anime, this is a fairly comedic scene in which Hisoka notices he’s been screwed over and then he leaves (In the 2011 Anime he just straight up goes into a super deformed state). Here, for some reason the scene is instead played straight, yet frankly it somewhat dragged out, with Hisoka’s body movements becoming ridiculous even by his standards. Worse yet, due to the way Takahashi Hiroki voices Hisoka, the scene is kinda hard to take seriously. It’s not that big a deal, but I do feel it’s something worth pointing out.

Animation:

It’s more or less on par with the late parts of 1999, if not a tad better. It’s crisp, fluid and more or less everything you could ask for. I’m still not a fan of 1999’ perpetually dark colors, but frankly Yorkshin’s last stretch is already one of the Manga’s more serious parts so I don’t really mind it that much in this particular case. I still prefer the way the 2011 Version looks, but I do get why this version has such a devoted fanbase.

Sound:

Most of the soundtrack is reused from the series, though the lack of any big actions scenes in this part of the plot did end up causing some of the more energetic tracks to be left out, which is kinda a shame, but whatever, the soundtrack was already great as it was. As for the Opening and Ending Themes, well, while I did really like “Carry On”, I didn’t particularly car for “Pale Ale”. It’s not a bad song per say, it’s just not my cup of tea.

Voice Acting:

Naturally, the entire cast returns and do a fantastic job as they always have. This would be Kaida Yuki’s last chance to do much of anything with Kurapika, and thankfully she did go out with a bang. While I didn’t mention him last time, I will say I did quite like Nagano Yoshikazu’s take on Chrollo. His voice is a bit too deep for my tast, but overall he certainly fits Chrollo’s whole “Soft Spoken yet otherworldly murderer” archetype. The rest (Bar Takahashi Hiroki) do a good job.

Overall:

Yeah, this OVA was great. It’s universally agreed that Yorkshin was the most lovingly adapted part of 1999, and it’s easy to see why. It’s tragic, dark, yet at the end there is a glimmer of much deserved hope. If you liked the 1999 Anime, I cannot recommend this OVA enough. It is legitimately that damn good.

Final Score: 10/10
