Synopsis

In Haikyuu!!: vs. Akaten, which takes place in the second season, Hinata and Kageyama fail a test and find that the make-up exam conflicts with their training camp.

Edit

Background

Haikyuu!! vs. Akaten (ハイキュー!! VS赤点) was screened at Jump Festa 2015 on November 3, 2015. It was later bundled with the 21st volume of the manga, released on May 2, 2016.

So, I'm guessing what this episode is is a section of the manga that was more fleshed out than what was shown in the series. In other words, this is an episode that they couldn't fit in the main lineup.

If you've seen the second season of Haikyuu!!, then you'll have seen certain parts of this episode already. All this episode does is fill in the blanks. I don't think it's an absolutely essential OVA to watch, but I do think that it provides a little something more to the characters.

Tanaka is the first example that comes to mind because you'll see him in a more serious light with this episode than what you'd normally see. You'll also hear him gives some honest opinions on the first years, which is rare. Tsukishima is the second example I have because you'll learn how he deals with his fellow first years and how much the freak duo really means to him as a member of the Karasuno volleyball team.

In other words, there is actually character discoveries in this episode, which is pretty cool for an OVA. Beyond that, you're just gonna get a funny episode of Haikyuu!! where you get to chill out with the characters without them being on the volleyball court. It's a nice extension to the bit of this episode that we see in the main anime, so if you really like the anime, definitely watch this.

Haikyuu! Season 1 OVA Review
By: Haruichi Furudate
July 7, 2019

There are two OVA's in this season. The first one is where a new character has been introduced. In this series, there's only two paths that they can choose from. It is to improve all members of the team or to add new member who have the foundation need and can add to the level of the team itself. And this OVA shows that they pick the first one. This character just need polishment and practice to improve. In the future, how he play will be a sight to see. The second OVA is more on a fun part. Volleyball is just an extra curricular activity. The main part is still studying and what mostly pains every characters in a story. It show how they struggle to pass all of the exams just to practice and focus more on volleyball. It's really just a funny and laughable OVA.
