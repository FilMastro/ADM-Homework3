Synopsis

Hinaria is unemployed, gaming every day. One day she decides to hack the servers belonging to the Beholder Group for some money, and she stumbles across a carefully secured record of two witches, Alice and Rimel. The two women wished to coexist with mankind, and the history they experienced is connected to the modern ZENONZARD.

This is gonna be a short review, and I'll just keep it condensed.

This short 9-episode OVA that has episodes come out every end of the month (from January to October, minus April) in the fable year of 2020 is nothing more but promotional material for the Zenonzard AI card game that's popular in Japan.

What this OVA entails are some of the many characters of their aptly-named Codeman pseudonyms, whom in these 9 episodes have shown, is more like an introduction to their characters and personalities that while may not have been derived from the actual game, the original script for them feels fine and nothing to rave about.

Contracting 8-bit to do this short OVA feels pretty good with consistent visuals, that's about it when it comes to visuals.

If there is one good thing about this short OVA, is the OST of a bopping OP and its many episodic EDs pertaining to the character of the month. Honestly, the best aspect for me.

Thankfully, Bandai Namco has since then released this game globally, so if this OVA has aroused your interest, it is now available for both Android and iOS to download to try for yourself.

Zenonzard is an anime based on the card game of the same name and while I expected a typical card game anime I got something different now is this good? Let's start the review spoiler free.The story starts as a typical card game anime but this is only for the episode zero and episode six the other episodes focuss on the other characters and it showing us different stories with them.Now the concept of zenonzard is really intreasting but for me it fails as a card game anime because it simply does not show the cardgame which it suppose to promote yes I can understand that the studio wanted to do something different but they didn't even try to link the stories and that let a lot of questions unanswered but probably the fact that the episodes last around seventeen minutes.But Zenonzard wasn't a complete failure as an anime since the variety of the stories were intreasting and enjoyable and actually it explores the whole AI theme with a very good way since we can see how AIs are watching the whole thing so yeah the plot was good and bad. The art is good and well-made.The opening is very good the voice acting was fine.The biggest part of the characters appear only for 1 episode and the variety of them were intreasting.Zenonzard is a wierd case of an anime while I enjoyed it as an anime it dissapoint me as a card game it's an intreasting look to the AIs but it litteratly forgot it's cardgame.I would recommend this anime to people who are fans of the AI but not to these who are funs of cardgame anime since it is something different from that.6/10
