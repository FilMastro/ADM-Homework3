Synopsis

It has been five years since Hiro's older brother passed away, and during those years, he has been living with his brother's widow, Yayoi. Fast forward to the present, Hiro's feelings for Yayoi are brighter than ever.

Will he be able to confess his feelings toward her? Will Yayoi be able to reciprocate said feelings?

Story
Hiro, who had been deeply in love with Yayoi-san, decided to change things for the better. After his older brother passed away, the love was brighter than ever. While on the other hand, Yayoi-san's feelings began to change with Doki Doki, but as happiness exists, sadness will be there too. Boku no Yayoi focuses on how one relationship began to crumble and how it will never be the same again in the end. While I am rather fond of this theme, it has been presented so badly that I can see this series skyrocketing to Gehenna itself. We can see many ups and downs in this series, such as a drastic change in characters' personalities, the feelings of loss, and how much better it is to be dead than dying. In all honesty, I just want to say enough. The plot is so unrealistic in a lot of ways, the characters' motivation is outrageous, and the whole stage blows life experiences like freaking trash. While this series is indeed hentai, and most watchers don't expect it much anyway, they still manage to make it way beyond 'bad'. In four words, it is freaking horrible.

Sound
To be honest, there is nothing special at all, but I do hear a voice like a granny who is reaching a climax before passing away. That's rather new though.

Character
Hiro, One hose vanilla boys, a little bit selfish as he wants to keep yayoi to himself, but still tolerable.

Yayoi, One of the few great waifu in hentai, but sadly turns into a pathetic bitch halfway. She had many chances to outwit a bad situation but chose to be a plaything instead. The stupid level probably reaches the god tier.

Doucebag, One of the shitty existences I've ever seen. He's ugly as a flea and his personality is just as shits. Cowardice, who always plays dirty, is probably better off dead than in the dinosaur age.

Conclusion
I enjoyed the first half of the show. It was so damn good and even if the romance was cliche as hell, it was still better. The second episode is the beginning of those stupid dramas, and the sequel just turned out to be worse. Better off dropping this series while you have the chance.

I went into this hentai with no expectations but I was pleasantly surprised by how strong of a start it has. Until 5 minutes after I realize it starts becoming utter dogshit which sucks when you have a hentai that started out with so much potential. Yes, today I will be reviewing the hentai: "Boku no Yayoi-san", strictly the first season as I don't even think I will bother with the second one. Without further ados, let's start.

Story: 3/10.
Now like I said, the starting for this hentai is very good. It has very good build up while keeping sexual interest for the viewer, and the first shmex scene while decent at best was still more rewarding for the immense build up it had. The premise is very strong and I feel the hentai knows what to do with this idea up until after the first shmex scene. After this first shmex scene (around 10 mins into the eps) the story just starts falling apart. It goes into a 'day by day' format after the first fu*k instead of building up a potentially string drama that could have taken place. This does upset me but pales in comparison to the crime that was episode 2. It is up to you whether you like NTR or now (for me it depends), but even if we ignore the NTR the episode was still fucking appealing. If the anime built the relationship between Yayoi and her brother-in-law then it could have made the hentai sooooo much better. Instead we get this bullshit episode that ruins the perfectly good premise presented at the start.

Art: 4/10.
I actually do find myself liking the art style quite a lot. The style is simple yet enticing with the animators choosing the perfect color palate and contrast for the hentai. However the praise ends here for this portion. The animation is not very good considering the time in which it was made. It is very choppy and has no fluidity to it all. To make matters even worse the directing is god-awful. The director did not know how to angle anything properly. Camera angles are very important in a hentai, as if you have too many angles on the face then it will become boringggg. And that is exactly what this hentai does and it really makes it hard (Puns xD) to do what you were trying to do from this hentai in the first place.

Sound: 7/10.
Sound is good. Of course it is not the best quality but they did a good job for the voice casting of Yayoi, that's about it. Sound is really the best part of this hentai which is not saying much as everything else does not stand up to the above 'ok' sound directing.

Character: 4/10.
As a character, I like Yayoi. She has a pretty good design and a captivating personalty, at least... FOR THE FIRST 5 MINUTES. I though she be able to handle things better but no. And also the reason she goes with the blonde dude legit make 0% SENSE!! WTF? Again she could have been a great character if it were not for the stupidity of the story.

Enjoyment: 3/10.
As you can tell, I did not enjoy this hentai. Normally I would be fine if the hentai was bad, I would just say it and move on. But this one has real potential at first until it really started wasting it. I doubt season 2 will be able to capture the first 5 minutes of this hentai. So I'll just end it off here.

Overall: 4/10. I would not recommend it...
