It's never a good thing to leave unexplained plot points, the spectator shouldn't be forced to theorize over unexplained stuff that can have many different explanations but don't make sense unless one of those possible explanations is actually shown. Same goes for the police showing up at the ex-boyfriend's house lol how did they find out the cellphone belonged to him? It's never shown to us, we're left to create a non-existing plot inside our heads, and that's not good storytelling. Still, I believe the general goal of the plot was fully achieved, and it was done fairly consistently, but it's impossible to ignore the inconsistent aspects, specially since the entire plot is based on the kidnapper not being found by the police for a long time, and that doesn't really make sense when there are literal recordings of him on the security cameras that every fucking konbini in Japan has, and if that specific one didn't, it should've been shown to the viewer. The last important thing I want to mention is the money situation. How did the kidnapper manage to live alone and pay his own bills when it's told he's unemployed? And if he wasn't, how would the anime handle the moments when the girl's alone? It would not only make much more sense for him to be employed, but it would also generate many plot possibilities of the girl maybe asking for help, or trying to escape, or even talking to herself and exposing her inner thoughts to the viewer through that, since we have no idea of what's going on in the girl's head (I'll elaborate on this later). This scene has different meanings depending on the kidnapper's thoughts. Also, it's never shown to the viewer what's going on inside the girl's head. It's impossible to know if her change of heart was just a plan for her to gain the kidnapper's trust and escape, or if she was genuinely starting to like him and like being used in general, or even if she was just going crazy. All interpretations are possible, and, in this case, this is not a good thing. This issue probably could've been fixed by not making the protagonist unemployed, since the girl would've some time alone to think and expose to the viewer what's her deal, and the same could've happened to the kidnapper while he's at work, leading to better understanding of their characters and better development overall. As I said before, tho, this would've probably annoyed people who just want to jack off, but needs to be pinpointed still.
