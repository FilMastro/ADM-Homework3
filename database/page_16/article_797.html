Synopsis

One will to create oceans. One will to summon the mulberry fields.

One will to slaughter countless devils. One will to eradicate innumerable immortals.

Only my will… is eternal.

A Will Eternal tells the tale of Bai Xiaochun, an endearing but exasperating young man who is driven primarily by his fear of death and desire to live forever, but who deeply values friendship and family.

(Source: Novel Updates)

Bai Xiao Chun is a rather inconsistent character who cowardly tries to avoid conflict above anything else. Throughout the show, he prefers to beg and flatter his way out of problems, and if that fails, he surrenders or runs. In fact, his whole motivation is to live as long as possible, and that's why he trains to be a sage-level cultivator in the first place, on the promises of immortality. This is all due to a traumatic event in his childhood, when his parents tell him that he must live on. While the show never fails to capitalize on the comedic factor of these moments, sometimes it could get frustrating, as if the narrative never progresses, because the protagonist is just trying as hard as he could to dodge all advancement.

However, there are a few times throughout the 52 episodes when Bai Xiao Chun is pushed into a corner and forced to fight. And here, he reveals that he's actually a genius martial artist that dominates enemies two levels higher than he is, despite never really having trained to fight in stress tested situations. He switches from silly to killer in a moment's trigger. Don't get me wrong. He trains hard in various combat skills. But it's hard to imagine him being good at fighting when he spends his life avoiding it. When he does fight, Yi Nian Yong Heng (A Will Eternal) shows off a brilliant high budget animation, one of the few 2D Chinese animations, and with very well directed action sequences that had me literally on the edge of my seat. As soon as I tell myself it's about to get good, our Student Bai goes back to his silly self.

Really, if there's any flaw with this show, it's the pacing. Despite being a cultivation show, it's really unlike any other. Instead of constant bullying and being forced to fend for one's life, the focus is, for the most part, more on training. Bai Xiao Chun spends the vast majority of the show either practicing his pill alchemy, making high level medicine for people, or practicing various cultivation skills and discerning their true meaning. Unlike in other cultivation shows, where the leveling are just vessels to push conflict, the leveling seems to be the whole point in Yi Nian Yong Heng. Therefore, to my dismay, the genre seems to be more of comedy and world building, with a little bit of adventure and slice of life, than an all out action-adventure archplot, which is what I wanted it to be. Despite so, I was able to appreciate its strengths for what they are. And while the moments between the action drag a bit -- let's be honest, they never, ever get as bad as standard bearing Japanese shounen anime like One Piece.

I will say that the comedy is rather funny, but it also happens that I sense it is stalling the plot, and I end up not enjoying it as much. And the training, despite it being drawn out, is actually one of the strengths of Chinese donghua, since unlike most anime, it actually shows serious attempts to train. In his spare time, Bai Xiao Chun is found reading books, memorizing herbs and their uses, practicing alchemy (which is a bit like blacksmithing, except with magical fire and herbal ingredients instead of weapons), and doing physical training to increase his level. In other words, he never skips out on training to sneak in to other people's summer training camps.

Anyways, take it for what you would, but while I would prefer that Bai Xiao Chun steps up to the plate more often so that the main plot points could move forward, I could also appreciate how the protagonist is spending his time to increase his level to a degree.

The other reviews mention a harem, but there really isn't one (although he ends up with one in the novel). The protagonist meets a couple girls that are potential romantic interests, but there is always one obviously primary interest, and never more than one girl per arc. The girls in prior arcs have a place in the story, but don't interact with Bai Xiao Chun much. If you're looking for it, you could convince yourself that the protagonist has it in the bag, but that is hardly the focus of the show. Except for main girl, none of the girls respect Bai Xiao Chun much for the majority of the arcs.

If you're looking for a good all-rounder with a more relaxed pacing, perhaps a bit of slice of life before the main plot picks up, then I'd easily recommend Yi Nian Yong Heng. But if you're looking for a power fantasy, you'd be sorely disappointed. While there are fight scenes to show Bai Xiao Chun's progress, most of the time he's coming up with petty little schemes to get out of jail free.

The series has quite a bit of drama, as expected from a Chinese show, but it's definitely much less than you would think.
It's HILARIOUS.
You will laugh throughout watching the whole series.
I saw in a review something about romance, so on that note, I would like to say that the ''romance'' part is after epp 48/49 when Bai (main character) changes his appearance because he is now older. Up until few last episodes he has the same appearance.
Love drama/romance is showed through elder Song Junwan (an elder of the middle peak of Blood Stream Sect). She falls for him and is annoyed that he is working in other peaks to refine pills.
There is a couple of scenes with them, but it is not so dominant.
Basically, until these last few epps you won't really see much of a romance, maybe here and there some girls who fall in love with Bai Xiaochun.
In the last episode, the ending is definitely showing that there is another season planned (the war and so on).
I didn't read the novel, but I found that Bai Xiaochun will have a couple of wives, so I think that next season/part will have much more romance than this one, because here he is mostly a child, until epp 48/49.
The main character's Bai Xiaochun personality is what makes this show so funny.
He is cunning, he is smart, he is sly, he does have some good intentions, he is not evil, but he does do everything for himself.
He does not plan to hurt others, it just so happens from time to time when he is practicing/refining pills or doing something.
What's interesting is that he has a divine beast from River-Defying sect and as seen in the anime he got himself a demon at Blood Stream Sect.
So he has 2 completely different pets.
He is really strong and capable, also very talented because he got enlightened with many sect secrets from different sects (well, I guess he is the main character for a reason).
Even though he is strong he prefers to act like he's weak and does not like to fight that much or to show off his true strenght.
He is really afraid of death and that's why he is practicing everything connected to imortality.

It's an interesting show and for me, it was really funny!
I started watching somewhere at the beginning around epp 10/12 because I taught it would have 25 epps, but then they changed it to 52.
I am glad that I watched it and I am looking forward to next season.
