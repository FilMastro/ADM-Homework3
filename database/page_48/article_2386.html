Synopsis

The delinquents of Ishiyama High School either fear Tatsumi Oga or wish to defeat him, but the young fighter finds himself preoccupied with other matters—like tending to baby Kaiser de Emperana "Beel" Beelzebub IV, the son of the Demon Lord.

Baby Beel's maid, Hildegard "Hilda," informs Oga that the infant has latched onto him due to the former's immense strength. In turn, Oga must take responsibility and raise the Demon Lord's son, or face Hilda's blade—that is, unless he can find someone stronger than him to appeal to Beel. Perhaps Oga can convince one of the Tohoshinki, composed of the four strongest fighters of Ishiyama High, to take Beel off his hands!

[Written by MAL Rewrite]

Edit

Background

Beelzebub: Hirotta Akachan wa Daimaou!? is the pilot episode of what would become the 60-episode series Beelzebub . The original video animation premiered at the Jump Super Anime Tour in Fukuoka, Japan, on October 23, 2010. It was subsequently released in DVD form along with the ninth volume of the manga on December 29, 2010.

Essentially this is just a preview of what's to come in the first 10-20 episodes of the anime. Don't bother watching this, just go straight to the anime.

If you already watched the anime, do not watch this.

This episode simply is meant to hype what at the time was the upcoming season, nothing more. And it does a good job of that, if you had previously read the manga. For those just getting into this, it might not make much sense. This essentially just spoils what are some very funny scenes and also actual plot.

The animation style of this is actually also a bit different than the anime.

It's pretty solid as a summary, but honestly it is not worth your time.

Eh, it was alright. Kinda generic, but I watched it contently. And I watched it in preparation for the full series. From what I understand, it's a preview of what's to come. But it's unique content. So if you're a fan of the show, there's no reason NOT to consume all the content you can.

This OVA was fast-paced, sure, but I can see that it was ultimately for the purpose of testing the waters for the full series. So it gets a "pacing pass" from me. But it's also a shame because the plot of this 30-minute episode could've been utilized for a span of like five 20-minute episodes: with the river and the Hilda fight being the first episode, and the next four being devoted to each of the "Tōhōshinki" members. (Maybe they do? I don't know, I haven't seen the main series yet. But, from what I hear, the OVA's plot occurs again.)

The concept is good. The setting is a school, but not really, because it's just a dilapidated building filled with grungy delinquents. Which again, is a unique concept, in itself. The cast is great, as I said -- full of different characters and diverse appearances. And I like the style in which they are drawn, too. I like the main character. The bad ass nice guys are great. The white-haired friend is a nice addition to the cast too. I can see great potential for his character. Anime babies are usually annoying but there's something very charming about Beel.

The animation is sub-par. I can tell they cut corners on action sequences. But other parts with skimpy animation (that appear to lack FPS) seem to have been utilized for comedic effect. And the comedy is decent. Nothing too funny, but I like the dynamic between the MC and Beel. That is where the comedy shines -- how the MC nonchalantly reacts to surreal situations.

I always hated Japanese voice acting for babies, because they usually use adult women and it's painfully obvious. Even if that is the case here, I frickin love the voice of Beezlebub. It sounds so realistic, like an actual infant. And it's actually really cute, which probably wasn't even their priority. (Note, it seems a lot of the VA's change for the main series.)

The OP's a bop. "Beel, Beel, Beel."

Overall, 6 or 7 out of 10. The anime (this OVA) was average, but charming in its own right, and deserves serialization/airtime. It's not something to get invested in -- assuming the entire show will be like this -- but it's something to watch idly. If anyone wants to kill some time, give this a watch. No background knowledge needed.

Also, note that this is chronologically the first animated content of the franchise. So, there's no reason not to watch it before the real show. Maybe it spoils some of the content, but don't we all spoil a little bit of a show for ourselves before watching it? (I.e. watching clips on YouTube or trailers.) Might as well sample the escargot before ordering the platter.

***Update, May 1, 2021***

I watched the entire anime series. All 50 episodes. It was garbage. I definitely jinxed myself with this review. I won't tell you not to watch it, but let me tell you I wish I could get the time I spent on it back.
